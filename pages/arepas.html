Arepas

When I was serving as a missionary in Chile I loved eating food from all over South America. There was food from Brasil, Peru and even Bolivia, but the food that I loved most came from the very northern coast of the continent. In Columbia and Venezuela, people love to eat Arepas, which are a type corn bread. Some people have described it as a really thick tortilla. This special bread is the perfect side to any meal! They are also relatively easy to make. When I moved to college, I was happy to find that in the city where I lived I could still buy the same corn meal! It turns out you can find it in most cities in the United States. For this reason, I am listing the brand of corn flour in the recipe.

    Ingredients

  • 1 cup Corn Flour (P.A.N. brand)
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • 1 pinch of salt

Heat up a pan at medium heat. Mix the ingredients, and roll the dough into a ball. Press the dough ball on the pan, flatening it to 1/2 inch. Cook until golden on the outside, and enjoy!

These simple arepas can be topped with chicken, cheese or any other tasty topping!
