Danish Pastries

My Grandma was from Denmark and from Denmark she brought this tasty recipe for pastries. When I was a boy, my dad would make these on Christmas and when I got a little bit older, I learned to make them too. Now I make them every few months and my neighbors at college all love them! I have people come up to me all the time and tell me that they want some next time I make some!

    Ingredients

  • 1/2 lb. margarine
  • 2 cups warm milk
  • 3/4 cup sugar
  • 2 pkgs. yeast
  • 1/2 cup water (for yeast)
  • 1 Tbs. sugar
  • 1 tsp. salt
  • 3 eggs
  • 6 cups flour

    Filling

  • 1 1/4 cups flour
  • 1/4 lb. margarine
  • 3/4 cup. sugar
  • 2 tsp. almond extract

Place margarine and sugar in bowl, pour heated milk over and let stand. Mix yeast with water and sugar and let stand 5 min. Add eggs and yeast to first mixture. Add salt and sifted flour. Beat together with spoon. No kneading. Cover with cloth and let rise in warm place for 1 1/2 hours. Punch down and let raise for 45 min. Roll and shape as desired. Let rise 30 min. Bake at 350 for 25 mins.
